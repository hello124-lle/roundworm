Fun Facts about Roundworms!

What Are Roundworms?

Roundworms, also known as nematodes, are tiny worms that can live inside the body. They're everywhere, and in fact, over 80,000 species exist! While they may seem a little creepy, learning about them can be both fun and important for healthcare professionals like nurses.

How Do Roundworms Affect Humans?

Roundworms can cause various infections, particularly in people who live in areas with poor sanitation. The most common type that infects humans is Ascaris lumbricoides, and it can cause stomach pain, coughing, and even intestinal blockage if left untreated. That's why it's important for nurses to spot symptoms early!

Ways to Prevent Infection

  • Wash your hands regularly, especially before eating!
  • Avoid consuming undercooked food or contaminated water.
  • Maintain clean and safe living conditions.
